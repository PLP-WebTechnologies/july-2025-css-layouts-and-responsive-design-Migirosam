Sidebar

Welcome to the sidebar! Here you can place important links, quick navigation tips, or even small announcements. A sidebar works well for secondary information that should be visible but not distracting from the main content.

For example, you might use this space for recent posts, popular articles, or a list of categories that help readers discover more content on your site.

Main Content

This is the heart of the webpage. The main content area is where you display your articles, blog posts, or product descriptions. Using CSS Grid allows us to position this section flexibly alongside the sidebar, while still adapting to smaller devices through media queries.

Responsive web design ensures that no matter the screen size—whether on a large desktop monitor, a tablet, or a mobile phone—your layout looks organized and easy to read. Flexible units like percentages and `rem` values help create designs that scale naturally.

Card 1

This card demonstrates the use of Flexbox for equal spacing and alignment. Cards like this are great for highlighting products, services, or features. They adjust automatically as the screen size changes.

Card 2

Another example card. Notice how it maintains balance and proportion. On smaller screens, these cards stack vertically for better readability, thanks to the `flex-wrap` property and responsive media queries.

Card 3

Cards can also contain images, buttons, or links. They are one of the most versatile design patterns used in modern web development, helping content remain both structured and visually appealing.
